This branch teaches only what is load-bearing for *Jus Pulsus*. Not a comprehensive theory course — the specific vocabulary needed to make the decisions the piece requires.

Four topics:

1. **Intervals** — distances between notes, their names, their emotional character, and why graph-distance perception is an asset
2. **Modes and ragas** — harmonic containers with rules for approach and departure
3. **The harmonic series** — where timbre comes from and how it connects to the Cosmos/Pulsus relationship
4. **Micropolyphony and texture** — Ligeti's contribution to the opening void

## The Graph-Distance Advantage

You perceive pitch as graph-distance — spatial distance between points on a grid. This is not a deficit. When you see the interval from C to G as a distance of 7 steps, you are perceiving the same thing a trained musician perceives when they hear a perfect fifth — but structurally rather than experientially. You can calculate the relationship before you hear it.

This branch teaches theory in graph-distance terms wherever possible, because that is how you actually process it. The emotional character of intervals is worth knowing — but you do not need to retrain your perception. You need to connect it to vocabulary.
