In a fugue, stretto occurs when a second voice enters with the subject before the first voice has finished. The voices overlap — the theme is being stated simultaneously at different points in its own timeline.

This creates extreme density. In Bach's most intense fugues, stretto with three or four voices produces an almost unbearable sense of compression and urgency. The voices are pursuing each other.

## This Is Your Ecstatic Tempest

The ecstatic tempest in *Jus Pulsus* is stretto at cosmological scale. Every character's theme entering before the previous character's theme has completed. Six or seven themes in simultaneous mid-statement. The music in multiple places at once.

## In Sonic Pi

```ruby
pitches   = [:e4, :g4, :a4, :b4, :d5]
durations = [0.5, 0.5, 0.25, 0.25, 1.0]

define :play_theme do
  pitches.zip(durations).each do |p, d|
    play p, sustain: d * 0.85, amp: 0.7
    sleep d
  end
end

# Three voices in stretto — each enters 1 beat after the last
live_loop :voice_1 do
  play_theme
  sleep 0.5
end

live_loop :voice_2 do
  sleep 1.0  # enters 1 beat late
  play_theme
  sleep 0.5
end

live_loop :voice_3 do
  sleep 2.0  # enters 2 beats late — tighter
  play_theme
  sleep 0.5
end
```

## Counterpoint: Independent Voices Together

Stretto is one kind of counterpoint. But counterpoint also means fully independent themes running together. In Act III, the Cosmos' string theme and Pulsus' brass theme run simultaneously. They were written independently. Together they produce a third thing neither of them is.

```ruby
cosmos_pitches = [:b3, :d4, :f#4, :a4]
cosmos_durs    = [1.0, 0.5, 0.5, 2.0]

pulsus_pitches = [:e2, :e2, :b2, :g#2]
pulsus_durs    = [0.5, 0.5, 1.0, 2.0]

live_loop :cosmos do
  cosmos_pitches.zip(cosmos_durs).each do |p, d|
    play p, sustain: d * 0.9, amp: 0.6
    sleep d
  end
end

live_loop :pulsus do
  pulsus_pitches.zip(pulsus_durs).each do |p, d|
    play p, sustain: d * 0.9, amp: 0.8
    sleep d
  end
end
```

Listen for what neither of them contains — the harmonies created at their intersections, the rhythmic interplay. That emergent third thing is not written anywhere in your code. It arises from the mathematics of the combination. This is the fugue. This is your piece.
