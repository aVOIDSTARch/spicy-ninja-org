This branch teaches compositional technique as a general discipline. The focus is on large-scale programmatic music — works where sound tells a story without words, where structure creates emotional inevitability, and where ninety minutes of continuous music stays coherent from beginning to end.

Five lessons:

1. **The song cycle and large-scale form** — how extended works accumulate emotional argument across multiple movements or sections
2. **Leitmotif architecture** — how recurring themes build a cast of characters, develop across a work, and create structural scarring
3. **Act structure and the density arc** — how large works build, sustain, and release tension; the role of density as a compositional parameter
4. **Emergence and orbital mechanics** — how independent simultaneous voices produce emergent complexity greater than any individual part
5. **The wound and its aftermath** — structural events that permanently alter everything that follows; how music encodes irreversibility

## The Discipline

Great programmatic composers — Prokofiev, Schubert, Beethoven, Reich, Coltrane — share one practice: they design systems that generate emotional logic, then commit to what those systems produce. They do not write every moment explicitly. They write the rules, the architecture, the constraints. The music follows from those decisions.

This course teaches how to think at that scale.

*Connection to practice: All techniques in this branch are illustrated with Sonic Pi examples and, where relevant, cross-referenced to compositional decisions in the project currently in development.*
