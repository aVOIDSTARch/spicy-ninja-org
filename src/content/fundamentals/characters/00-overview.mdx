A character in programmatic music is not a label on a theme. It is a complete entity with sonic identity, narrative function, relational structure, and predictable behavior under pressure.

A character is not compositionally viable until you know them well enough that their response to disruption is predictable. Can you answer: if the central crisis of the work occurs while this character is mid-phrase, what do they do? If not, the character is not finished.

## Why Character Development Is Compositional Work

The decision that a specific timbre *is* a specific character — not represents them, but *is* them — is one of the most consequential compositional decisions in a programmatic work. It determines how the character develops, what transformations are possible for them, and how the listener relates to them.

Prokofiev matched timbral personality to narrative personality with surgical precision. The wolf is three French horns in unison not because horns are large (though they are) but because the specific character of a horn in its lower register — heavy, implacable, slightly threatening even at medium volume — IS the wolf's personality. They are the same thing expressed in two different media.

This precision is the standard.

## The Five Dimensions of a Complete Character

1. **Sonic identity** — timbre, register, rhythmic signature, harmonic character, dynamic behavior, spatial position
2. **Narrative identity** — role in the work, emergence, development arc, relationship to the central crisis, final state
3. **Relational identity** — who is their structural counterpart, who is their ally, who creates productive tension
4. **The witness function** — how other characters (especially commentary figures like a Greek chorus) respond to them
5. **The interview** — ten questions that cannot be answered analytically; answered in the body before the mind

## The Course Structure

Four lessons:

1. **Sonic identity and timbral casting** — how to choose a sound that IS a character
2. **The development arc** — how a character changes across a large work, and why transformation requires sufficient cause
3. **The superpartner relationship** — structural pairing in programmatic music; the deep coupling between two characters
4. **The interview** — the ten questions, how to use them, and what the answers generate

Each lesson uses worked examples drawn from the classical and electronic tradition, with a running example from a work in development.
