## The Creation Myth

**CORE** — A 90-minute minimum programmatic electronic suite structured as a personal creation myth. The work is not about music — the universe *is* music. Consciousness, structure, and matter are musical phenomena. The listener experiences a cosmogony. `[A]`

**CORE** — The piece is an EDM interpretation of a personal creation myth, not a genre exercise. The electronic language is the native tongue of this story, not a costume over classical forms. `[A]`

**CORE** — The structural and philosophical method is inherited from Prokofiev's *Peter and the Wolf*: each character in the myth is represented by a specific sonic identity — timbre, rhythm, register, harmonic character. These are leitmotifs in the electronic domain. `[A]` / `[B: Prokofiev / Wagner]`

**CORE** — The work must function on multiple simultaneous registers — physical/vibrational, emotional, structural, mythological, musicological — such that every listener regardless of background or ability has a complete and valid experience. The deaf raver principle: felt before heard. `[A]`

**LIKELY** — Duration: 90 minutes minimum. Possibly extending to 2 hours depending on narrative requirements. Not to be padded — only as long as the story demands. `[A]`

**LIKELY** — The work is conceived as a suite in four acts (see structure document for full outline). `[C: S1-003]`

## Structural Principles

**CORE** — **Supersymmetry**: Every character has a superpartner — not a simple opposite but a character with deep structural relationship. Paired at the level of physics, not surface aesthetics. `[C: S1-007]`

**CORE** — **The Opening Duality**: The piece begins with two characters at the extreme edges of human auditory perception. A sub-bass pulse (felt more than heard, infrasonic threshold) and a sparse high bell (barely present, almost imagined). Between them: a void that feels full. These two never fully leave the piece. `[A]`

**CORE** — **Emergent Complexity**: Characters emerge gradually, combining in 20% increments of possible combinations until most of the cast is present. No character announces itself fully formed. The cosmogony is a process of organization, not a series of arrivals. `[A]`

**CORE** — **The Kintsugi Wound**: At some point in the narrative a character dies or transforms irreversibly. Their leitmotif is not removed — it is *scarred*. A note that used to resolve no longer does. A rhythm gains a ghost offbeat. The piece cannot return to what it was before, and this is audible. The golden seam is visible and it is beautiful. `[C: S1-004]`

**CORE** — **The Ecstatic Tempest**: A climactic convergence where all or most leitmotifs are simultaneously present, interlocking, generating emergent complexity. Modeled on Sufi sema mathematics — disciplined ecstasy, not chaos. The rhythm underneath should be irregular (7/4 or 9/8 candidates) so the ecstasy feels uncontainable but not random. This event causes harm or transformation — a plot point, not just a climax. `[A]` / `[B: Sufi / sema tradition]`

**LIKELY** — The ending strips back to the original two characters — bass and bell — transformed by the journey but recognizable as what they always were. The gravitational center that never moved. `[C: S1-010]`

## Philosophical Layers

**CORE** — **Polyphonic Depth**: The work operates simultaneously on multiple registers — physical, emotional, structural, mythological, musicological — each complete in itself but compounding exponentially for those who perceive additional layers. No layer is required. All layers reward.

**CORE** — **The Joseph Campbell Monomyth**: The hero's journey underlies the narrative arc. Ordinary World (void), Call (pattern organizing), Road of Trials (characters developing and clashing), Ordeal (closest to collapse into noise), Return (transformed themes — the bass and bell heard again carrying the weight of everything).

**LIKELY** — *Peter and the Wolf* homage is structural and philosophical — the method is inherited, not the material. The piece is not a remix or an adaptation. It is a descendant.

**LIKELY** — Tolkien's *Ainulindalë* as direct spiritual reference `[A]` / `[B: Tolkien]` — the void, the music that organizes it, the consciousness that *is* the structure. The Silmarillion passage describing the void and the music that organizes it is the emotional template for the opening act.

**LIKELY** — Nada Brahma `[B: Hindu philosophy]` ("the world is sound") as philosophical underpinning — the universe doesn't *have* music, it *is* music.

## World Music Integration

**CORE** — **African Drumming**: All music began in Africa. This character receives prominent and reverent treatment — longer than other world music homages. Every other character responds to its introduction as if hearing their grandmother's cooking for the first time. Recognition before cognition. This beat was born there. `[A]`

**CORE** — World music intrusions feel like *visitations* — a character temporarily illuminated or possessed by another tradition, not a genre switch. Each intrusion lasts long enough to make its point then the character's original identity reasserts — changed but recognizable.

**LIKELY** — Indian classical intrusion: a character adopts a raga framework briefly. Bhairav suggested — flattened second and sixth, simultaneously ancient and searingly emotional to Western ears.

**LIKELY** — Some intrusions fizzle — the character attempts the new idiom, stumbles, retreats. This is intentional and honest. Not all encounters change you. The fizzle is itself a respectful acknowledgment.
