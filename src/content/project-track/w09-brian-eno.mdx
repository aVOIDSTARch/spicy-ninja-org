Eno invented ambient music — not as background wallpaper but as a specific philosophical position. The liner notes to *Music for Airports* describe the ideal ambient music as "able to accommodate many levels of listening attention without enforcing one in particular; it must be as ignorable as it is interesting."

This is your polyphonic depth principle stated from the composer's side. Every layer of your work — physical, emotional, structural, mythological, musicological — must work at the ignorable level before it can work at the interesting level.

## Discreet Music: The Mathematics of Loops

*Discreet Music* is made from two tape loops of different lengths running simultaneously — not identical lengths, so their relationship is always slightly shifting, producing new harmonic relationships at every moment that were never composed. This is the same mathematics as Reich's phasing but at a different tempo and applied to melody rather than rhythm.

Your Void Static works on this principle. The Ground and the Bell are loops of different effective lengths whose combinations produce patterns nobody wrote.

## Music for Airports: What Sparsity Does

Piece 1/1 is a single sustained note, a few fragmentary phrases, repeated in an order determined by loop lengths' mathematical relationship. It is unassuming and then, after fifteen minutes, quietly devastating.

Study the mechanism of that devastation. Nothing was added. Nothing escalated. The sparsity created the space for the listener to bring their own weight to the material. This is your kintsugi moment's environment — not the moment itself but the space around it.
