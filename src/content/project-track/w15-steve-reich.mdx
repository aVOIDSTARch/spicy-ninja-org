Steve Reich studied West African drumming in Ghana in 1970 and returned to build a new kind of Western music from what he learned. *Drumming* is 90 minutes of drums and voices, built from a single 12-beat pattern that is gradually shifted out of phase with itself.

## Phasing: The Core Mechanism

**Phasing** — two copies of the same pattern starting together, then one gradually pulling ahead until they re-align. The emergent patterns between the two out-of-phase copies are never played by anyone. They arise from the mathematics of the offset.

In Sonic Pi terms: if `live_loop :a` runs a 12-step pattern and `live_loop :b` runs the same pattern starting `sleep 1` later, the *combination* produces a third thing that neither loop contains. This is how your orbital mechanics work.

## Your Specific Mathematics

Your three simultaneous drumming cycles — 3-beat, 4-beat, 7-beat — produce emergent patterns at every moment whose length is LCM(3, 4, 7) = **84 beats** before the pattern repeats. You composed a pattern 84 beats long by writing three patterns of 3, 4, and 7. The 84-beat cycle is not in your code. It arises from the mathematical relationship between your loops.

Reich uses this to create structures that take 20 minutes to complete one full cycle. Your orbital mechanics may create cycles that span an entire act.

## Music for 18 Musicians: Applied Scaling

*Music for 18 Musicians* applies the same thinking to pitched instruments. It sounds like a living, breathing ecosystem — not complicated, all simple patterns slightly offset from each other, their combination producing something that sounds like weather.

This is your Act III at full character density. Not chaos — a living system whose behavior is determined by the mathematical relationships between its elements.
