A Markov chain is a system where the probability of the next state depends only on the current state. Applied to music: given the current note is C, what is the probability the next note is E, G, or Bb? Trained on enough musical examples, a Markov chain generates melodies that sound plausible without knowing anything about music theory.

This is both useful and philosophically important. It suggests that musical coherence — the feeling that a sequence of notes belongs together — can emerge from local statistical regularities without global planning. The coherence is emergent rather than designed.

## Emergence as compositional principle

In *Jus Pulsus*, Act III depends on emergence: multiple characters each following their own trajectories, with orbital periods that gradually converge, producing a supernova of combined density that no individual character planned. The characters do not coordinate. The catastrophe emerges from their separate motions.

Markov chains are the tool for prototyping these emergent textures in Sonic Pi before the full compositional work begins.
