A raga is not a scale. It is a personality — a set of notes, yes, but also the specific ways those notes are approached and left, the ornaments that are characteristic, the time of day when the raga is appropriate, the emotional state it embodies. A performer does not play a raga. They inhabit it.

This specificity — the idea that musical material carries non-negotiable emotional and contextual associations — is directly relevant to the leitmotif architecture of *Jus Pulsus*. Each character's leitmotif is not just a memorable tune. It is a bundle of sonic and structural properties that can be transformed (wounded, scarred, developed) while remaining identifiable.

## Tala and the rhythmic framework

The tala system — rhythmic cycles of varying lengths, felt rather than counted — is one of the most sophisticated rhythmic frameworks in any musical tradition. A sixteen-beat cycle (teentaal) has its own internal organisation, its own weight distribution, its own sense of where the gravity lives. Ravi Shankar's recordings on the Project Track syllabus demonstrate this with exceptional clarity.
