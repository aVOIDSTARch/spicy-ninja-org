Before melody, before harmony, before timbre — there is the grid. Rhythm is the first music because it is the simplest statement of time divided by intention.

This root asks a single question: what does it mean to hold time? Not to count it — any metronome counts time — but to hold it, to embody it, to become the reason other voices know where they are.

This is the entry point for understanding *Jus Pulsus* from the ground up. Pulsus is not a character who plays rhythm. Pulsus *is* the pulse — the BPM, the meter, the grid itself. Every other voice exists in relation to what Pulsus provides.

## Where this leads

From here the tree opens toward **African Drumming** — the oldest technology of collective rhythm, polyrhythm as democratic conversation — and **Electronic Lineage** — the moment rhythm was synthesised, sequenced, and handed to machines while retaining its humanity.

Both branches are different answers to the same root question.
