A vibrating string does not produce one pitch. It produces an infinite series of frequencies in fixed mathematical ratios — the fundamental, its octave, its fifth, its fourth, its third, and so on down to the edge of audibility and beyond. This series is not a human invention. It is physics. It is also, not coincidentally, the harmonic vocabulary that every musical tradition on earth has converged upon independently.

The relationship between mathematics and music is not metaphorical. It is constitutive. Intervals are ratios. Consonance and dissonance are products of wave interference patterns. The reason a perfect fifth sounds stable and a tritone sounds unstable is a matter of acoustics first and culture second.

## Why this matters for the project

Understanding the physics of sound is the precondition for understanding why Bach's voice-leading rules are not arbitrary — they are mappings of acoustic reality onto compositional practice. It is also the precondition for understanding why Markov chains can generate plausible melodies without knowing anything about music: they are modelling statistical regularities that emerge from acoustic physics.

From this root the tree opens toward **Fugue and Counterpoint**, **Markov and Emergence**, and **Sufi Mathematics** — three different ways of thinking about structure in sound.
