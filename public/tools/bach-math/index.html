The Number Alphabet ( Gematria )

In Baroque Germany, letters carried numerical values — a tradition inherited from Hebrew gematria and practiced openly in theological music. Bach encoded his own name into compositions as a signature and a devotional act simultaneously. It is not conjecture: musicologists have documented hundreds of instances across the oeuvre.

Key insight: BACH = B(2) + A(1) + C(3) + H(8) = 14. The number 14 appears in the final unfinished contrapunctus of Art of Fugue — the subject spells B-A-C-H just as Bach dies, leaving the fugue with 14 entries. Whether accident or design, it functions as a sealed door.

B♭ – A – C – B♮  (German pitch names: B = B♭, H = B♮)

Fugue Architecture

A fugue is not a form in the way a sonata is a form — it is a texture, a set of compositional procedures. The mathematical interest is that it operates on a single idea (the subject) through systematic transformation. The complexity is generated, not composed independently.

Mathematical structure: The four standard fugal transformations form a mathematical group under composition — Original (P), Inversion (I), Retrograde (R), Retrograde Inversion (RI). P∘P = P, P∘I = I, etc. The same group structure underlies the twelve-tone rows Schoenberg invented two centuries later. Bach was doing group theory before the mathematics existed.

The Interval System ( Consonance & Dissonance )

Baroque counterpoint is governed by a codified hierarchy of intervals. The rules are not arbitrary — they map to the physics of the overtone series. Perfect consonances appear early in the harmonic series; dissonances appear later or not at all. Bach's mastery was knowing exactly when to violate the rules and why.

The Six Rules of First Species Counterpoint

First species is note-against-note. These rules created the harmonic vocabulary Bach worked within — and creatively violated.

Invertible counterpoint: Bach frequently wrote counterpoint designed so the upper and lower voices can be swapped and the result remains musically correct. This requires that every interval between the voices also be valid when inverted. A 3rd inverts to a 6th (3+6=9); a 5th inverts to a 4th. This is a severe constraint Bach treated as a design challenge.

The Four Transformations ( The Group )

Bach's compositional transformations are not ornamental — they are the engine. Every fugue subject can be subjected to four operations that together form a mathematical group (closure, associativity, identity, inverses). This is the same abstract structure as a rectangle's symmetries. Bach intuited group theory two hundred years before Galois formalized it.

Every combination of two transformations yields another in the set. P is the identity element.

Crab canon (Canon cancrizans): The ultimate case — a piece that sounds identical when played backwards. Bach included one in the Musical Offering (BWV 1079). The notation itself forms a visual palindrome. He sent it to Frederick the Great as a demonstration that a single theme could generate infinite music.

Proportional Architecture ( φ = 1.618… )

The golden ratio φ is the proportion where A/B = (A+B)/A = 1.618… It arises from the Fibonacci sequence — each term divided by the previous approaches φ. Whether Bach consciously deployed this proportion or whether it emerges from his structural intuition is genuinely debated. The occurrences are too frequent to dismiss.
